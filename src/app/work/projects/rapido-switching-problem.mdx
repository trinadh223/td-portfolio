## Overview

A comprehensive product teardown analysis of Rapido's user switching problem, focusing on understanding why users switch between ride-hailing platforms and identifying opportunities to improve retention.

## Problem Statement

Rapido was experiencing significant user churn with customers frequently switching to competitor platforms. This analysis aimed to identify the root causes and propose actionable solutions.

## Approach

- **User Research**: Conducted in-depth research to understand user behavior patterns and pain points
- **Competitive Analysis**: Analyzed competitor offerings and their value propositions
- **Data Analysis**: Examined user journey data to identify drop-off points
- **Root Cause Analysis**: Applied first principle thinking to understand core issues

## Key Insights

1. **Price Sensitivity**: Users showed high sensitivity to price fluctuations during peak hours
2. **Availability Issues**: Inconsistent bike availability in certain areas led to platform switching
3. **Experience Gaps**: Identified specific UX friction points in the booking flow
4. **Trust Factors**: Safety and reliability concerns influenced platform choice

## Recommendations

Proposed targeted solutions addressing:
- Dynamic pricing transparency
- Supply-side optimization strategies
- UX improvements for faster booking
- Trust-building features and safety enhancements

## Outcome

This analysis secured a **top 2 ranking** among 80+ participants in the Upraised Product Management cohort, receiving recognition from senior product leaders at Mastercard, CRED, PayTM, and Myntra.
